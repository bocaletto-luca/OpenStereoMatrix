Overview

This single-page document contains the complete project: rationale, safety, schematics (drawn in SVG), wiring steps, recommended values, bill of materials, and licensing. It provides a professional, reproducible approach to derive a phantom center, ambience rears, and an active sub feed from standard stereo amplifier outputs.

What this is

  • A passive, reversible matrix for 2.0 amplifiers
  • HTML-only documentation with SVG schematics
  • Center via L+R; rears via L−R; sub via divider

What this is not

  • Not discrete surround (no Dolby/DTS decode)
  • No DSP, no active mixing (unless separately added)

Safety & compatibility

  • Common-ground vs BTL/Class‑D: many modern amps are bridge-tied (per-channel floating). Never tie negative terminals together on non–common-ground amplifiers.
  • Phantom center requires a common ground reference. If unsure, avoid center wiring and use only the Hafler rears and sub feed with isolation.
  • Respect minimum load. Avoid dropping below the amplifier’s specified impedance.
  • Resistors in summing/attenuation paths dissipate heat. Use adequate power ratings and ventilation.
  • Start at low volume; check temperature rise after 15–30 minutes; adjust values if necessary.

When in doubt, use the Hafler rears (do not tie negatives) and an active sub with isolated high-level inputs or transformer isolation.

Quick start

  1. Confirm amplifier topology (common ground or BTL/Class‑D).
  2. Select a mode (2.1, 3.0, 4.0, 5.0, 5.1) and review its schematic below.
  3. Prepare resistors and connectors per the “Recommended values” and “BOM”.
  4. Wire on a terminal block or inside a ventilated box. Double-check polarity.
  5. Power on at minimum volume. Verify function and balance. Increase gradually.

Modes & schematics (HTML/SVG)

Mode 2.0 — Direct stereo (reference)

Baseline connection. Front L and R driven directly by the stereo amplifier. No matrix elements used.

Mode 2.1 — Active subwoofer feed

Derive a line-level signal from speaker outputs using a high-impedance divider. Feed an active sub’s RCA line-in (stereo) or a summed mono input.

  • Per channel: 10 kΩ series to node → 1 kΩ to ground → RCA tip. RCA sleeve to ground.
  • Mono sum: two 10 kΩ (from L and R) into one node, then 1 kΩ to ground → single RCA.
  • For BTL/Class‑D amplifiers, the sub must provide isolated inputs or use transformer isolation.

For mono subs, sum L and R with two 10 kΩ resistors into a single RCA tip before the 1 kΩ to ground.

Mode 3.0 — Phantom center (L+R)

Derive a center channel by summing L and R through equal power resistors. Requires a common-ground amplifier.

  • L+ → 8.2 Ω (15–25 W) → Center+
  • R+ → 8.2 Ω (15–25 W) → Center+
  • Optional shunt across Center: 8.2–10 Ω (25 W) for level stabilization
  • Center− to common ground (only if the amplifier is common-ground)

Increase summing resistor values for a softer center. The optional shunt lowers center impedance and level; adjust to taste.

Mode 4.0 — Hafler rears (L−R ambience)

Rear speakers connect between L+ and R+ so they reproduce the difference signal. This emphasizes ambience and out-of-phase content naturally present in many stereo recordings.

  • Each rear wired between L+ and R+ (no connection to negatives).
  • Optional attenuation per rear: 3.3–10 Ω series resistor (10–25 W) or an 8 Ω L‑pad (50 W).
  • Typically compatible with BTL/Class‑D amps (negatives remain separate), but always verify documentation.

Rear level should be subtle. If rears feel dominant, increase the series resistor or fit an L‑pad to trim.

Mode 5.0 — Center + Hafler rears

Combine the phantom center (L+R) and Hafler rears (L−R) for a convincing spatial field. Each section can be switchable.

Switching can be done with DPDT/4PDT toggles or a rotary selector to enable/disable center and rears independently.

Mode 5.1 — Add active sub

Mode 5.0 plus the 2.1 divider feeding an active subwoofer. Not discrete 5.1, but highly effective for music and general content.

Set sub crossover and phase by ear or measurement; typical ranges: 60–100 Hz crossover, 0/180° or variable phase to integrate with fronts.

Bill of materials (BOM)

  • Power resistors: 8.2 Ω (15–25 W), 3.3–10 Ω (10–25 W), optional 8.2–10 Ω (25 W) shunt
  • L‑pad attenuators (optional): 8 Ω / 50 W for rear level control
  • Resistors for sub divider: 10 kΩ and 1 kΩ (¼ W), quantity per chosen topology
  • Terminal posts/binding posts: IN L/R, OUT Front L/R, OUT Center, OUT Rear L/R
  • RCA connectors: Sub L/R or Mono
  • Switches (optional): DPDT or 4PDT toggles, or rotary selector for mode control
  • Enclosure (vented) or terminal strip board; heat‑resistant mounting hardware
  • Wire: appropriate AWG for expected current; heat‑shrink, labels

Switching & physical layout (guidance)

  • Keep high-current speaker runs short and twisted for each polarity to reduce hum pickup.
  • Mount power resistors with airflow and clearance from plastics; consider small heatsinks.
  • Use separate switches for Center and Rears. A third switch can enable the Sub divider.
  • Label all terminals clearly: IN L/R, FRONT L/R, CENTER, REAR L/R, SUB OUT.

Impedance & simple math

  • Two 8 Ω speakers in parallel: R = (8×8)/(8+8) = 4 Ω
  • Two 8 Ω in series: R = 8 + 8 = 16 Ω
  • Avoid total load below amplifier minimum (commonly 4–8 Ω for many consumer amps).
  • Resistor power estimate: P = V²/R. Use headroom and derating for safety.

The Hafler network typically adds modest loading because rears see the difference signal, but thermal checks remain essential.

Testing & tuning

  1. Begin with 2.0 to verify baseline operation.
  2. Add the Sub divider (2.1). Confirm low noise and proper sub gain; set crossover/phase.
  3. Enable the Center (3.0). Adjust summing/shunt values to anchor dialogue without collapsing width.
  4. Enable the Rears (4.0). Trim level (series resistor or L‑pad) until ambience is perceptible but not distracting.
  5. Combine to 5.0/5.1. Recheck amplifier temperature and resistor heating after 20–30 minutes.

If any component becomes excessively hot to the touch, increase power ratings or adjust levels/values. Never exceed the amplifier’s load specifications.
